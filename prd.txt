PRD: Prism (MVP v1)1. Executive Summary
Prism is a privacy-first, on-device decision companion that helps users understand and improve how they make decisions.
Unlike traditional productivity or reflection tools, Prism does not analyze content (emails, messages, documents) or keystrokes. Instead, it passively observes lightweight behavioral signals (app usage, switching patterns, time context) to identify potential decision moments.
Through a low-friction feedback loop—micro-confirmations and end-of-day outcome tagging—Prism builds a dataset of decision patterns and delivers a weekly insight connecting:
Behavior + Feeling + Outcome → Actionable Pattern
The MVP goal is not perfect detection, but to reliably generate a trusted “aha moment” within 3–5 days.
2. Problem Statement
Knowledge workers and founders operate in high-noise environments with constant context switching, interruptions, and time pressure.
Key issues:
Decisions are made unconsciously and under suboptimal conditions
Reflection is inconsistent, biased, or absent
No system exists to connect how decisions are made with how they turn out
Existing tools are invasive (content tracking) or high-friction (journaling)
Result:
Users lack a reliable feedback loop to improve decision quality.
3. Goals & ObjectivesPrimary Goal

6. User Stories
As a user, I want Prism to automatically detect potential decision moments so I don’t have to log them manually.
As a user, I want to label decisions in under 2 seconds so it doesn’t interrupt my workflow.
As a user, I want to review outcomes quickly at the end of the day.
As a user, I want a weekly summary that clearly explains how my behavior impacts decision quality.
As a user, I want the system to feel private and non-invasive so I trust it.
